# SOCIAL_STRATA_AND_PATHWAYS

This document defines the main social positions and how people move between them.

## Core rule
These are not isolated job trees.
They are social positions within the same world.

## 1. Clan / family
The player’s primary layer.
This owns:
- lineage
- marriage
- inheritance
- support allocation
- prestige
- branch tension

A person can remain within family authority while also participating in exams, trade, office, force, or outlaw paths.

## 2. Commoners / households
The social base.
Includes:
- tenant farmers
- laborers
- shop hands
- petty traders
- servants
- migrants
- local militia potential

This layer supplies:
- labor
- pressure
- vulnerability
- the population base from which many later paths emerge

## 3. Exams
Institutional upward mobility.
Path pressure:
- family investment
- local school access
- tutor quality
- time
- stress
- rank competition

Outcomes:
- advancement
- stagnation
- withdrawal
- return to family/trade responsibilities
- later bridge into office

## 4. Trade
Wealth/network mobility.
Path pressure:
- capital
- route safety
- market volatility
- trust networks
- debt
- logistics

Outcomes:
- prosperity
- leverage
- debt dependence
- alliance opportunities
- family support capacity

## 5. Office
Formal authority pathway.
Path pressure:
- exam success or patronage
- reputation
- family strategy
- political risk

Outcomes:
- authority access
- legal leverage
- higher exposure
- family tension through distance, posting, or political danger

## 6. Outlaw / banditry
Disorder and coercive edge pathway.
This is not a “cool faction mode”.
It emerges from:
- famine
- debt collapse
- expulsion
- war damage
- failed upward mobility
- revenge pressure
- security breakdown

Outcomes:
- route pressure
- coercive power
- black-route access
- recruitment or suppression pressure
- long-term stain, fear, and retaliation

## Typical transformations
- commoner -> exam aspirant
- trade wealth -> family-funded education
- exam failure -> trade or household duty
- office loss -> household return, exile, or factional decline
- debt collapse -> outlaw risk
- war devastation -> migration, outlawing, or militia dependence
- family expulsion -> trade exile, office exile, or outlaw path

## Design rule
These transitions should be produced by world pressure and module logic, not by hard-coded career trees.
